There is a debate whether English 'will' is a future tense or a modal. On the one hand, we find 'will' frequently appearing in contexts which express futurity. On the other hand, we also see that 'will' does not behave like other tense operators in English, which puts it into question about whether 'will' should be treated as a tense operator. In this project, I provide arguments about why 'will' should be treated as a modal rather than a tense operator. Then, I leverage on evidence from Korean to explain why and how can modals express futurity.
