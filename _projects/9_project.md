Korean loanwords such as 'oppa (brother)' are used very often by the young generation in China. These words convey cultural implications and can have a potential impact on how their users perceive Korean culture. Studying the usage and influence of loanwords can provide insights for culture propogation. This project conducts a survey in Shanghai International University to study whether undergraduate students use Korean loanwords, where do they learn these loanwords, and how using these words affects their perceptions of Korean culture. 

It is found that students generally understand and use some Korean loanwords, and they mostly learn these words from Korean drama, K-Pop shows, and Chinese social media. They also report using these loanwords has a positive impact on how they perceive Korean culture. It is therefore suggested that policy makers should pay more attention on these venues for more efficient culture propogation.
